Adventures

The Wonders Beneath the Waves

DIVING IN UNAWATUNA

The waters that border Unawatuna’s beaches harbour an amazing variety of natural beauty and biodiversity – the best way to get up-close and experience all this wonder is to dive right in! Diving in Unawatuna is one of the best things to do here in Sri Lanka’s South Coast, so don’t pass up on the opportunity during your stay here at the Araliya Beach Resort and Spa.

The coral reefs that fringe Unawatuna’s shoreline will be your first stop. These are complex ecosystems that provide a refuge for some of the most beautiful, fascinating and downright bizarre creatures found on the planet. As you explore the reefs, you’ll come across colourful denizens like the parrotfish and the reclusive moray eel. Get lucky and you might even catch a glimpse of the rare and exotic sea turtles that are known to frequent these waters!

Keep in mind that diving is a somewhat tricky affair and you’ll need adequate training before jumping into the depths of the ocean. Luckily, there’s plenty of diving schools to be found here in the South Coast including one at our resort itself!







The Hidden Beauty

HORTON PLAINS NATIONAL PARK

Looking for hotels near Horton Plains? Experience one of the most popular places to visit in Nuwara Eliya with Araliya Resorts & Spas. A hike through this spectacular wilderness is a must-do for anyone who is vacationing in Sri Lanka’s misty hill country. From spectacular landscapes to unique wildlife sightings, the Horton Plains is full of exciting adventures and memories that are just waiting to be discovered.

There are two famous attractions waiting to be discovered here – the sheer drop that has been appropriately named the World’s End and the picturesque Baker’s Falls. The World’s End is perhaps the main reason so many tourists and travellers flock to this national park. Reaching it involves quite a challenging hike, but the reward waiting for you is worth it. The view you get from this cliffside is truly unprecedented – the mountains mists will often blanket the sprawling landscape for miles on end.

Baker’s Falls is another great site to visit while you’re here – it’s often touted as one of the most beautiful waterfalls you can find in Sri Lanka. There are lots of great photo opportunities to get in on here so do remember to bring a camera!

Finally, there’re the residents of the Horton Plains. You’ll find all kinds of wonderful wildlife here from giant squirrels to an array of endemic bird varieties. The sambar deer is a common sight here at the park, and most will even venture up to you in search of a free snack. Keep your eyes peeled along the underbrush though, the island’s top predator – the Sri Lankan leopard – also call this wilderness home.







Ancient Rock Fortress

SIGIRIYA ROCK FORTRESS

Being one of the eight enchanting world heritage sites in Sri Lanka, the ancient rock fortress of Sigiriya is a famous and well renowned historic attraction on the island. Surrounded by well-landscaped water and rock gardens the fortress offers its visitors so much to see and explore. The site, with innumerable offerings, comes in as an attraction that you should explore at a relaxed and slow pace.

The fortress, also being known as the lion rock was once the seat of King Kashyapa. Many legends and chronicles state that the king was a man who loved art hence he wanted Sigiriya the culmination of intricate and elaborate varieties of different art forms. Though many artistic creations have been destroyed by the test of time and during foreign invasions, some remain patiently praising the artistic allure of a bygone era. The main draw to the rock fortress is its frescoes which offer the onlookers glimpses of the king’s harem. There are numerous ruins in and around the main edifice which would have once been elaborate courtyards, palaces, and places of worship.

It is indeed exciting and exhilarating to explore the premises and the fact that everything around is drenched in history inspires awe in your mind. The water gardens of Sigiriya are also another significant draw of the site which provides evidence for the existence of an advanced hydraulic civilisation during an era where modern machinery was completely absent. There are ruins of fountains, pools, and ponds, many of their intricate mechanisms still function. Excursions to this amazing attraction can be arranged by reaching out to our resort’s front desk.
